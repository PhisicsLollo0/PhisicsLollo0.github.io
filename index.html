About Me

I strongly believe in science communication as an opportunity to build a direct bridge of dialogue between science and society. This would help break down the ivory tower in which scientists have been secluded for centuries and bring science into the heart of the public. The recent global pandemic and the alarming proliferation of conspiracy theories have starkly underscored the pressing need for continued efforts in this regard. Around 8 years ago I founded a community of popular science (in Italian) called "Io Amo La Scienza" (I love Science) whose Facebook page counts around 50k followers.

Throughout my time at university and my doctoral studies, I've realized the vital importance of actively engaging in societal and institutional politics as a scientist. I have consistently taken on an active role as a student representative, both during my Master's and doctoral studies. Daily, I passionately advocate for human rights, actively combating racism, sexism, ableism, and homo/trans-phobia.

Research Interests

My primary research interests include quantum algorithm scalability, quantum machine learning, and secure quantum communication networks. I am currently exploring the intersection of quantum computing and artificial intelligence to pioneer the next generation of AI applications.
